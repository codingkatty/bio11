Inherited Diseases

Inherited diseases are genetic disorders that are passed down from parents to their children through genes. These diseases can be caused by mutations in a single gene, multiple genes, or a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Below is a chart summarizing some common inherited diseases, their causes, symptoms, and inheritance patterns.

Disease Causes Symptoms Inheritance Pattern
Down Syndrome Trisomy 21 (an extra copy of chromosome 21) Intellectual disability, characteristic facial features, and increased risk of heart defects. Chromosomal disorder (nondisjunction)
Turner Syndrome Missing or incomplete X chromosome in females Short stature, delayed puberty, and infertility. Chromosomal disorder (monosomy)
Klinefelter Syndrome Extra X chromosome in males (XXY) Reduced testosterone levels, infertility, and breast tissue development. Chromosomal disorder (nondisjunction)
Hemophilia Deficiency of clotting factors (usually factor VIII or IX) Excessive bleeding, easy bruising, and joint pain. X-linked recessive
Cystic Fibrosis Mutation in the CFTR gene affecting chloride channels Thick mucus in lungs, digestive issues, and respiratory infections. Autosomal recessive
Sickle Cell Disease Mutation in the HBB gene leading to abnormal hemoglobin Chronic pain, anemia, and increased risk of infections. Autosomal recessive
Color Blindness Deficiency in color-detecting pigments in the retina Difficulty distinguishing between certain colors (usually red and green). X-linked recessive

Conclusion

Understanding inherited diseases is crucial for diagnosis, treatment, and genetic counseling. Genetic testing can help identify carriers of genetic disorders and provide information for family planning.
